Christianity is one of the three major abrahamic religions. The christian movement started around the time after Jesus's death and has grown to be one of the most influential and most practivced religions. Christians adhere to the bible as their main source of doctrine but the actual teachings may vary from sect to sect. The main belief is that Jesus Christ is the son of God and that through him salvation is found.
Islam is one of the fastest growing religions in the world with over a billion followers and counting. Islam is the second largest religion and also considered an abrahamic religion. Islam's core belief is that there is one god which is Allah in arabic. Through God's final prophet Muhammad the word of God was given which is known to be the Quran. Through the Quran and Muhammad's teaching muslims are able to connect to God.
Buddhism was founded by Siddhartha Gautama around 2,500 years ago in India. Buddhism is one of the largest religions in the world. The teachings of buddhism aim to release suffering from those who follow. Through the main teachings of the Buddha we learn that pain and suffering stems from desire and that by releasing one's self from that desire or attachment can lead toward a better life without suffering.
